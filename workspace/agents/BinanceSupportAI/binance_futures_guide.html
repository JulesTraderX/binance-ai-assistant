📘 1. What Are Binance Futures?

Binance Futures allows you to trade cryptocurrency contracts with leverage — meaning you can control a position larger than your actual capital. There are two main types:


  • USD-M Futures: Settled in USDT/BUSD. Most popular. Ideal for stable returns in USD value.
  • COIN-M Futures: Settled in the underlying cryptocurrency (BTC, ETH...). Better if you want to accumulate crypto.
⚙️ 2. How to Get Started

1 Activate your Futures account

  • Go to Binance → Derivatives → USD-M Futures
  • Accept terms & conditions
  • Pass the knowledge quiz (mandatory)

2 Fund your Futures wallet

  • Transfer USDT from Spot wallet to Futures wallet
  • Menu: Wallet → Transfer → Spot → Futures
  • Minimum recommended: $50–$100 to start safely

3 Configure your settings

  • Margin mode: Isolated (safer for beginners) vs Cross (shares entire wallet)
  • Leverage: Start with 2x–5x maximum when learning
  • Asset mode: Single-asset or Multi-asset (advanced)
🔑 3. Key Concepts to Master

📊 Long vs Short

  • Long: Bet the price goes UP. Profit when price rises.
  • Short: Bet the price goes DOWN. Profit when price falls.

⚡ Leverage

  • 10x leverage = control $1000 with $100
  • Amplifies profits AND losses equally
  • Higher leverage = liquidation risk closer

💧 Margin

  • Initial margin: Capital required to open position
  • Maintenance margin: Minimum to keep it open
  • Below maintenance = liquidation

💸 Funding Rate

  • Fee paid between longs and shorts every 8h
  • Positive rate: longs pay shorts
  • Negative rate: shorts pay longs

📉 Liquidation

  • When margin drops below maintenance level
  • Position is automatically closed
  • Use stop-loss to avoid this

📋 Order Types

  • Market: Instant execution
  • Limit: Execute at your target price
  • Stop-Market / Stop-Limit: Risk management
📈 4. Common Trading Approaches

🎯 Trend Following

Trade in the direction of the dominant trend. Use moving averages (MA20, MA50, MA200) to identify trend direction. Long in uptrends, short in downtrends.

📊 Support & Resistance

Identify key price levels where the market repeatedly reverses. Buy near support, sell/short near resistance. Use order book depth to confirm levels.

⚡ Scalping

Take many small profits throughout the day. Uses 1m–5m charts. Requires discipline and fast execution. High frequency, small leverage (3x–5x max).

🏗️ Swing Trading

Hold positions for days or weeks to capture larger moves. Uses 4h–1D charts. Requires patience. Less affected by short-term noise.

🛡️ 5. Risk Management — The Most Important Section
❌ Most traders lose money not because of bad strategy, but because of poor risk management.
Rule Recommended Why
Risk per trade 1–2% of capital Survive a losing streak
Leverage 2x–10x max Avoid fast liquidation
Stop-Loss Always set one Limits max loss per trade
Take-Profit Set before entering Lock profits automatically
Risk/Reward ratio Minimum 1:2 Profitable even at 40% win rate
Daily loss limit Max 5–10% per day Stop trading when in bad shape
✅ Golden Rule: Set your Stop-Loss BEFORE opening the position. Never move it further against you.
